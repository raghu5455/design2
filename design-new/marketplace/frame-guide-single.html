Understanding Conservation Framing: What You Need to Know

    • Subscribe

April 07, 20233 min read

Conservation framing is a process that aims to preserve the artwork and protect it from damage caused by environmental factors. This involves using high-quality materials and techniques that prevent the artwork from deteriorating over time. In this article, we'll explore what conservation framing is, why it's important, and the key factors to consider when selecting a framer who specialises in conservation framing.

What is Conservation Framing?

Conservation framing is a process that involves using archival-quality materials and techniques to frame the artwork. The goal of conservation framing is to protect the artwork from damage caused by environmental factors such as light, humidity, and pollutants. The materials used in conservation framing are specifically chosen for their ability to protect artwork from these factors.

Why is Conservation Framing Important?

Conservation framing is important because it helps to preserve the artwork for future generations. Without proper framing, artwork can deteriorate over time and lose its value. Conservation framing ensures that the artwork is protected from environmental factors that can cause damage, such as fading, yellowing, and warping.

Key Factors to Consider When Selecting a Conservation Framer:

1. Expertise

When selecting a conservation framer, it's important to choose someone who has expertise in the field. Look for a framer who has experience in conservation framing and has received specialised training in this area. This will ensure that your artwork is properly protected and preserved.

2. Materials

The materials used in conservation framing are critical to its success. Look for a framer who uses archival-quality materials, including acid-free matting, UV-protective glass, and museum-quality frames. These materials will help to prevent damage to your artwork and ensure its longevity.

3. Techniques

Conservation framing requires specific techniques to ensure that the artwork is properly protected. Look for a framer who uses techniques such as hinging, which secures the artwork to the matting without damaging it, and spacers, which keep the glass away from the artwork to prevent moisture buildup. These techniques are important for ensuring that the artwork is properly protected and preserved.

4. Certification

Look for a framer who is certified by a reputable organization, such as the Professional Picture Framers Association (PPFA). This certification ensures that the framer has received specialized training in conservation framing and adheres to strict standards of quality and expertise.

Conclusion

Conservation framing is an important process that helps to preserve the artwork and protect it from environmental factors that can cause damage. When selecting a framer who specialises in conservation framing, it's important to consider their expertise, materials, techniques, and certification. By choosing a framer who is experienced in conservation framing and uses high- quality materials and techniques, you can ensure that your artwork is properly protected and preserved for future generations.
